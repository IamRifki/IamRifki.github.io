How I view programs.

Preface

Hello everyone, today's post is ramble of mine about how I view programs and programming.

DISCLAIMER: I do not claim to be some language theorist or an amazing programmer, I'm just a person who's finding their way into programming, and their views on it.

Ramble

In my view, Programming is nothing but a tool to solve a problem that I have.

There's several parts to programming. Understanding the hardware, understanding languages, and developing ways to solve problems effectively like in mathematics.

What is fundamentally a program?

Algorithms + Data Structures = Program.

When programming, you need to understand the purpose of what you are doing it for. If you then understand the purpose, then you need to understand how it should function. If you then understand its function, then you need to know how it will be used. If you then understand its usage, then you can worry about the implementation or form of it.

Most of my time is not programming code, what I called the implementation.

Most of my time are dealt with the previous aspects.

Purpose -> Function -> Usage -> Form. Understand this, and its direction.

You cannot start anywhere.

Conclusion

Again, this is just a ramble of mine and shouldn't really be used as a reference on how to code, then again, some of the people I look up to follow this principle, and it worked for them so, I guess I'll follow in their footsteps.
