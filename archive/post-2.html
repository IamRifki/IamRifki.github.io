The joy of programming.

Preface

When I say write open source code for free to any of my IRL friends or family, they always say,

"Why do you code for free, can't you profit from it by selling it and making it closed sourced?"

I simply laughed and say "It's the joy of programming that matters most", then they get confused and ask "What is the joy of programming?"

Joyous Programming

The joy of programming comes to this, are you happy or are enjoying yourself when you are programming?, this applies to both programming for business and for yourself.

It seems like companies don't and won't recognize that programmers need to have joy in what they're making or they lose interest fast.

This is very different to say Hobbyware projects

Hobbyware is a term I use to describe software written by a couple of people with no other purpose than for fun and learning. most of my programs I write are like that.

That is why most maintainers of FOSS projects still maintain code for free, because they have fun while programming, although, sadly companies are taking advantage of open source by using their code commercially and not giving back a single nickel or contribution.

Back to Topic.

The reason why I still love releasing my code for free is because I want to have fun, and help other people learn programming and have fun their way.

To me, it's a win-win for sure, I don't have any hate towards closed source software, because they exist for a reason, and you shouldn't force people to open their code, but there's just this beauty that open source has that makes me want to keep opening my code, just the thought that I could help someone with something by opening my code is just wonderful.

And that to me is the Joy of Programming, thank you very much.

- IamRifki
